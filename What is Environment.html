What is Environment

Introduction

The natural surrounding that all the organisms exist in is called the environment, the living and non-living components of the earth and how they interact with one another. Understanding the concept of the environment is highly essential because it helps us know and appreciates the importance of preserving the world we live in and the impact we have on it.

The environment not just helps us exist, but provides us with various benefits such as ecosystem services, and resources for our survival and well-being. Even if one of the components of the environment is skipped, it may pose a threat to all living and non-living beings in one way or the other. This makes understanding the Environment more important to save it from any such potential dangers.

This blog focuses on providing a deep understanding of what the environment requires, the importance of conserving it, and the responsibilities we have as individuals to ensure we safeguard it for future generations and invest in sustainability.

Understanding the Components of the Environment

The environment is not a single unit but a collection of various components that come together to form the ecosystem. The various components that form the environment are listed hereafter.

Natural environment

The foremost component of the Environment consists of naturally occurring resources that can be renewable, or non-renewable. These make up the basic structure of the ecosystem and provide the necessities for survival.

1.     Land

Land or terrestrial area is the most widely occupied part of the environment as it provides a home to many species like humans, mammals, and plantations. The land is not only essential to help the survival of living beings but also plays a significant role in regulating our climate, air, and water quality. However, it has been suffering lately due to the increasing human population, increased levels of deforestation, land degradation, and pollution. This threatens the sustainability of land resources and we must take the necessary steps to conserve and manage our land resources for the sake of future generations.

2.     Water

Water is another crucial component of the environment. Not only it supports the survival of human beings, but it also supports the growth of flora and fauna. Water also plays a crucial role in shaping the landscape and regulating our climate. However, water resources are increasingly scarce and at risk due to climate change, pollution, and overexploitation.

3.     Air

Air is that component of the environment that no other component can survive without, it supports the landscape, the terrestrial, and aquatic life. The air contains all types of gases that support various life forms like nitrogen for bacteria, carbon dioxide for plants, and oxygen for humans, birds, and animals. Without air, none of these life forms would survive and thus, it makes the conservation of quality of air highly efficient for all life forms.

4.     Biodiversity

Biodiversity is an essential component of the environment that encompasses the coexistence of species of plants, animals, and microorganisms in the environment. It is important in maintaining the ecological balance and sustaining healthy life on Earth. It is thus important to know, conserve and protect biodiversity for the benefit of current and future generations.

 Built environment

Apart from the Natural environment, the growth in human population has led to the development of Built environments on Land, Water, and even in space. These have caused stability in human settlement and consist of the following components.

1.    Rural and Urban areas

There are two types of human settlements, the Rural and Urban areas. Rural areas are those which are not much developed in terms of technology but are connected to nature to support human life. Urban areas, on the other hand, are technologically developed and support a comforting lifestyle for the overall development of people.

2.     Infrastructure

Infrastructure means the physical stricture of a place. This includes the town layouts, roads, and buildings that determine how well-developed the particular area is. The infrastructure of any area, be it an Urban or Rural area, is an important component of the human-built environment and contributes to the development of society.

3.     Human-made structures

Human-made structures such as buildings, hospitals, and schools are some contributing factors to the Environment. These structures play a vital role in the development of human settlements, providing safe places for people to live, work, and learn. The design of these structures has evolved to reflect changing human needs, such as the move towards sustainable and eco-friendly building practices.

Interactions within the Environment

While there exist various components within the environment, their interaction is what makes a completely functional environment suitable for existence. These interactions can be various depending on the factors they contain and the way they affect it all.

1.    Ecosystems

Ecosystems are essential components of the environment that contain all the living organisms in a specific region, as well as the non-living elements they interact with. For example, a coral reef, which houses a diverse range of organisms such as fish, plants, and crustaceans can be termed as an ecosystem. The importance of ecosystems lies in their contribution to ecological balance and stability, providing various services such as clean air and water, food, and climate regulation. Without the interaction, balance, and stability of these ecosystems, many organisms and human societies would struggle to survive.

2.    Environmental factors

Environmental factors play a vital role in determining the suitability of an ecosystem for the existence of living organisms. Climate and weather patterns heavily influence an ecosystem by dictating the temperature and precipitation levels, which can severely impact the organisms' ability to survive. Soil composition and fertility are also critical factors that determine the types of life present in an ecosystem, while the availability of resources such as water, food, and shelter further shapes the ecosystem's composition. Without these environmental factors, the delicate balance between living organisms and their environment would be disrupted, potentially leading to the collapse of the whole system.

The Role of Humans in the Environment

Humans have been a part of the environment for centuries and have affected and influenced it in many ways, either intentionally, or unintentionally. This has also negatively affected the environment and its other components of it in multiple ways that need to be identified and reduced to keep the environment safe and sustainable.

Human Impact on the Environment

1.     Industrial activities

Humans have developed industries for various purposes that have helped the human race in growth and development. However, it has been noticed that not all industries are safe for the environment and to this day, the majority of industries do not follow the basic safety standards and as a result, harm the ecosystem and its components.

2.     Deforestation and land degradation

Humans, for settlement and industries, have been clearing lands of trees and causing deforestation for a while now. This deforestation and degradation of the environment have been causing an imbalance in nature affecting the carbon sinks, oxygen cycle, and other species as well. While the human race requires settlement areas, other creatures require forest batches to survive which should be kept untouched to help co-existence.

3.     Pollution and waste

The growth in human population has been on a constant rise which has caused rapid growth in resource requirement, pollution, and waste generation as well. While the majority of this waste can be managed with effective waste management, some of these are also non-manageable and cause permanent damage to the environment like non-biodegradable waste or synthetic waste which are tough to be broken down by natural components.

 Environmental consequences of human actions

1.    Climate change

Human activities such as industrialization and urbanization have affected the climate severely. This is because of the release of harmful chemicals and gases that are not supposed to be present in the atmosphere in such high concentrations. The trial of technologies has been causing global warming, depletion of ozone, and changes in rainfall and climate patterns that put many components of the environment in grave danger.

2.     Loss of biodiversity

Human actions such as deforestation, pollution of natural ecosystems, and poaching have led to many species getting endangered or even extinct. The loss of any species from the ecosystem is not normal and acceptable as it can cause a ruckus in the entire food chain, cause some species to overgrow and others to go extinct and the concept of co-existence fails miserably.

3.     Resource depletion

Humans have been using natural and artificial resources to the fullest without paying attention to the need for sustainability or future generations. This has been causing a gradual depletion of natural resources such as Coal, Petroleum, Natural gases, Timber, and other natural resources that may take thousands of years to get renewed naturally. This rate of depletion may cause future generations to live in such scarcity as much as it is harming the environment.

To Preserve and Protect the Environment

The current practices by humans have been impacting the environment in such a way that it is not too long before the entire environment faces scarcity in the basic needs of life and is increasing the chances of calamities. Thus, it is required that we take steps to preserve and protect the environment to make it a safer place for all its creatures.

 Ecological preserving

1.     Clean air and water

The most essential yet negatively affected components of the ecosystem are air and water which support the fundamental requirements of all life forms. The first step in preserving the environment is to reduce and reverse the effects of pollution on air and water and take steps to keep them clean and healthy for the use of all organisms.

2.     Biodiversity and ecosystem services

Biodiversity is the co-existence between species and ensuring biodiversity will make sure all organisms get their fair share of the environment and no component is overpowered by the other as this may cause an imbalance in nature. The environment must function as per the laws of biodiversity to ensure the proper working of ecosystem services.

3.     Climate regulation

The climate of the Earth has also been affected much by pollution, deforestation, and development in townships. This has caused the summers to be longer, winters to arrive later and go by faster, and rains to be poisonous instead of cleansing. Such effects of pollution must be reduced to make sure that the climate is regulated back into its original state and nurture the environment instead of harming it.

 Human well-being and health

1.    Access to clean resources

While humans are the reason for the imbalance of the ecosystem, they are also being affected by its side effects. They have access to resources but have lost access to clean resources. Instead, they are all now surrounded by pollution and contamination that are causing various health hazards like breathing issues, water-borne diseases, skin problems, and other health issues that can only be fixed by a clean environment.

2.    Psychological and physical benefits of nature

A polluted environment poses a threat to all living animals whereas a clean environment and being closer to nature positively affects them, psychologically and physically. Organisms leaving in clean environments are often seen to be healthier and have lower risks of emotional or physical diseases.

3.    Sustainable livelihoods

Sustainability is the main focus of environmentalists today. Sustainability refers to living in the ecosystem and using resources in such a way that it does not get replenished and stays intact for future generations as well. Ensuring sustainability in all aspects of life will help in fixing the environment and make it a safer place to exist.

 Conclusion

In conclusion, the environment plays a vital role in sustaining all life forms that include microbes, animals, birds, humans, flora, and fauna, and it's crucial to pay attention to its conservation. As mentioned earlier, a polluted environment poses a significant threat to human and animal health, leading to various diseases and other health-related issues. Therefore, a collective effort should be made towards promoting a sustainable future, which would involve sustainable livelihoods, environmental conservation, and responsible resource management. We should all take responsibility for preserving the environment and work towards creating a healthier and safer environment for future generations.
