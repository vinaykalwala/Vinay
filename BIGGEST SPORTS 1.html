1.Indian Premier League (IPL)

 

Countries: India

Administrator: BCCI

Format: T20

Tournament format: Double round-robin and playoffs

Number of teams: 8

Current champion: Mumbai Indians (5th title)

First edition: 2008

Latest edition: 2021

 

The first competition, which lasted 44 days in 2008, was won by the Rajasthan Royals, a team from a lesser market, under the leadership of the legendary Australian bowler Shane Warne. Following the IPL's popularity, other cricketing nations raced to create their own domestic T20 tournaments in an effort to share in the wealth.

 

With its inception in 2008, the tournament not only revolutionised the face of the game, but it is also one of the largest leagues in the entire athletic globe. According to Duff & Phelps, a consultancy firm, the IPL is currently one of the most valuable brands, with a brand value of $6.3 billion in 2018. The competition has not only revolutionised the game, but it has also had an impact on India's economy, adding $11.5 billion to the country's GDP in 2015.

 

Vivo, the current title sponsor of the IPL, inked a five-year contract of $2,199 crores ($341 million) in 2018, which is 11 times the sponsorship costs given by DLF in 2008.

 

The IPL also lets its franchises to spend a lot of money on player auctions, with each team receiving an 80 crore purse. This means that 640 crores ($91.9 million) may be spent only on players, a significant increase above the BBL and other major leagues. In 2019, prize money totaled 50 crores ($7.2 million), with the Mumbai Indians alone pocketing 20 crores.

 

Now for the hen that lays the golden eggs - broadcasting rights! Star India purchased the IPL's broadcasting rights for five years in a deal of 16,347.5 crores ($2.55 billion). Without a question, this is the most costly broadcasting deal in cricket history. However, it also made the IPL the third richest league in terms of money earned each game, trailing only the NBA and the NFL.

 

2.Ram Slam T20 Cricket League/Betway Cricket League

 

Cricket Leagues: (Credits MSC Sports)

Countries: South Africa

Administrator: Cricket South Africa

Format: Twenty20

Tournament format: Double round-robin, qualifier, and final

Number of teams: 6

Current champion: Imperial Lions (4th title)

First edition: 2003–04

Latest edition: 2020–21

 

Ram Slam T20 Challenge is South Africa's local tournament, and it was founded in 2003-04. It is one of the oldest T20 Cricket Leagues in the world. The tournament's 15th edition, held in April-May 2019, was the final one as Cricket South Africa prepares to revamp their domestic cricket league.

 

The competition acquired worldwide acclaim as a result of the participation of T20 specialists such as AB de Villiers, David Miller, and others. The exact details for the competition's income and sponsorship contract are unknown. However, with title sponsors Ram, the major courier firm, and coverage on Super Sports, the Ram Slam T20 Challenge is unquestionably one of the top seven richest leagues.

 

3.Big Bash League (BBL)

 

Countries: Australia

Administrator: Cricket Australia

Format:         Twenty20

Tournament format: Double round-robin and knockout finals

Number of teams: 8

Current champion: Sydney Sixers (3rd title)

First edition: 2011–12

Latest edition: 2020–21

 

The Big Bash League is Australia's gift to the cricket world. It is the world's second most famous T20 League and one of the biggest attractions on the cricketing calendar. The league was founded in 2011 and has grown steadily over the last eight seasons. Cricket Australia was also the first governing body to launch the Women's T20 League, known as the Women's Big Bash League (WBBL).

 

Broadcasting is clearly the most lucrative source of revenue for BLL in the sports-crazed nation. The rights were retained by Network Ten until 2018 for a fee of $100 million. Cricket Australia, on the other hand, struck a $1.12 billion agreement with Fox Cricket and Seven Network last year for their whole cricketing season. Despite the fact that this figure encompasses everything from the domestic circuit to international contests,BBL is undoubtedly one of the primary causes of the skyrocketing money.

 

CA also raised the prize money from the previous season, bringing the total to $890,000 for the top four. A further $600,000 is allocated to the clubs rather than the players. The total salary cap increased by $1.6 million in 2016.

 

4.Caribbean Premier League (CPL)

 

Countries: West Indies

Administrator: Cricket West Indies (CWI)

Format:         Twenty20

Next edition: 2021

Tournament format: League and play-offs

Number of teams: 6

Current champion: Trinbago Knight Riders (4th title)

First edition: 2013

Latest edition: 2020

 

West Indies' comeback in T20 cricket came after a slump in test and ODI cricket. When it comes to the shortest version of the game, the two-time World T20 champions are now the finest in the business. The Caribbeans boast some of the best players in the T20 format in Chris Gayle, Pollard, Andre Russell, and Dwayne Bravo. As a result, it's no surprise that they established their own league in 2013.

 

As of 2018, CPL's estimated brand value was over $102 million, and its current title sponsor is Hero MotoCorp, India's top automobile brand. The rivalry has grown in prominence as a result of Shahrukh Khan's investment in the Trinbago Knight Riders.

 

Each franchise in the auction is eligible for a purse of $600,000 as part of the competition. The overall prize money for each season of the CPL is roughly $1 million.

 

5.Natwest T20 Blast

 

Countries: England

Administrator: ECB

Format: Twenty20

Tournament format: Group stage and knockout

Number of teams: 18

Current champion: Notts Outlaws (2nd title)

First edition: 2003

Latest edition: 2020

 

Due to sponsorship considerations, the oldest running T20 competition, the Twenty20 Cup or Natwest T20 Blast, is England's largest domestic competition in cricket's shortest format. Unlike other leagues, the ECB has maintained a priority on giving grassroots players a chance in the competition.

 

The number of teams competing in the competition is also significantly more than in other leagues, with 18 clubs from England and Wales in total. Teams are given a total purse of $45 million, with prize money ranging from $2.6 million to $2.6 million.

 

Sky Sports, the UK's premier sports broadcaster, has paid $120 million for the rights to broadcast the T20 Blast. Although this figure appears to be more than that of most other Cricket leagues throughout the world, it is actually a steal.

 

6.Bangladesh Premier League (BPL)

 

Cricket Leagues: (Credits Sportzwiki)

Countries: Bangladesh

Administrator: BCB

Format: Twenty20

Next edition: 2022

Tournament format: Round-robin and Playoffs

Number of teams: 7

Current champion: Rajshahi Royals (1st title)

First edition: 2012

Latest edition: 2019–20

 

Bangladesh, a cricket-crazy country, decided to follow in the footsteps of a number of other countries by establishing its own T20 Cricket League. The first season of the BPL was held in 2012, following a 350-crore pact between the Bangladesh Cricket Board and Game On Sports Group.

 

After six seasons, the franchise-based tournament, which presently features seven teams, is one of the most popular Cricket Leagues. The competition's seventh iteration will begin in December 2019.

 

In 2016-17, BPL earned $2.6 million in sponsorships and made $51 million in gross income. Since 2017, GTV and Maasranga TV have secured the broadcasting rights for BPL for $76 million.

 

7.Pakistan Super League (PSL)

 

Countries: Pakistan

Administrator: PCB

Format: Twenty20

First edition: 2016

Latest edition: 2021

Tournament format: Double round-robin and playoffs

Number of teams: 6

Current champion: Karachi Kings (1st title)

 

Despite challenges with hosting games in Pakistan, the PCB has done an admirable job of operating the PSL for four seasons. The competition began in 2016 when the commercial rights were sold to the first five franchisees for $93 million over a 10-year period.

 

According to Arib Habib, CEO of one of Pakistan's leading corporate corporations, the PSL has developed at a breakneck pace since then, with its market value surpassing $300 million in 2017. The PCB also reported a $2.6 million profit in its inaugural season.

 

The competition's title sponsor has been Pakistan-based Habib Bank Limited (HBL) since its inception, and the contract was renewed in 2019 for $14.3 million over a three-year period.

 

PCB spends $1 million in prize money and gives its six franchises a total reward of $7.2 million. The current broadcasting rights are held by PTV Sports and Geo Super for a reported $36 million.

 

8.SA20 League

 

Countries: South Africa

Administrator: Cricket South Africa

Format: Twenty20

Tournament format: Round-robin and playoffs

Number of teams: 6

Current Champion: Sunrisers Eastern Cape(1 st title

First Edition: 2022-23

Latest Edition: 2022-24

 

Cricket Six franchise owners have been named for the SA20, which will begin on January 10. All six South African franchises were successfully bid for by Indian Premier League franchise owners.

 

Six teams—the Durban's Super Giants at Kingsmead, the Joburg Super Kings at Wanderers, MI Cape Town at Newlands, the Paarl Royals at Boland Park, the Pretoria Capitals at Centurion, and the Sunrisers Eastern Cape at St. George's Park—will compete in a total of 33 games.

 

Professional men's T20 league called SA20 will feature well-known players from all around the world. The broadcaster SuperSport owns a 30% share in SA20, while former IPL Chief Operating Officer Sundar Raman owns the remaining 20%. CSA is the league's largest shareholder, owning 50% of league shares. The SA20 League appears to be quite successful in its inaugural season since cricket fans are paying close attention to it. Players of the highest calibre from all over the world have expressed interest in the inaugural South Africa League. On January 10, SA20 Season 1 will debut with an impressive opening ceremony.

 

9.Lanka Premier League (LPL)

 

Countries: Srilanka

Administrator: Srilanka Cricket

Format: Twenty20

Tournament format: Double Round-robin and playoffs

Number of teams: 5

Current Champion: Jaffna Kings(3rd title)

First Edition: 2020

Latest Edition: 2022

 

The highest domestic T20 event in Sri Lanka, the Lanka Premier League (LPL) 2022, which will feature a number of top international talents, is slated to take place from December 6 to December 23.

 

The LPL 2022 was initially planned to run from August 1 to August 21, however just weeks before the opening game, the tournament was indefinitely postponed due to the nation's economic difficulties.

 

The league stage of the six-team tournament will now begin on December 6 and end on December 23, according to the updated schedule.

 

Five teams will compete in the competition for the prized title in Sri Lanka: Colombo Stars, Dambulla Aura, Galle Gladiators, Jaffna Kings, and Kandy Falcons. Double round-robin matches pit each team against the other.

 

There will be a total of 24 matches, with 20 in the league and four in the playoff round, including the championship match. The LPL 2022 was to be held at three different locations: R. Premadasa Stadium (RPICS) in Colombo, Mahinda Rajapaksa International Cricket Stadium (MRICS) in Hambantota, and Pallekele International Cricket Stadium (PICS) in Pallekele.

 

While the MRICS, Hambantota will host the first few league phase games in the 5-team competition, the action will then transfer to PICS, Pallekele, and lastly to RPICS, Colombo for a few league phase matches and the playoff rounds.

 

Angelo Matthews, Wanindu Hasaranga, Dasun Shanaka, and Thisara Perera are among the Sri Lankans on the pitch, while Fabian Allen, Shoaib Malik, and Romario Shepherd are expected to play.

 

10.Afghanistan Premier League (APL)

 

Countries: Afghanistan

Administrator: Afghanistan Cricket Board

Format: Twenty20

Tournament format: Double Round-robin and knockout

Number of teams: 5

Current Champion: Balkh Legends (1 st title)

First Edition: 2018

Latest Edition: 2018

 

Afghanistan Premier League (APL) is the country's domestic T20 cricket championship. The Afghanistan Cricket Board (ACB) is in charge of the Gulbahar Afghanistan Premier League. Due to internal conflict and security concerns in Afghanistan, the competition was hosted in the United Arab Emirates (UAE).

 

The inaugural APL took place from October 5 to October 21, 2018 at the iconic Sharjah Cricket Stadium. Over forty international players competed alongside Afghan nationals in the competition. In August 2018, the ICC approved and gave the go-ahead for the Afghanistan Premier League T20 plans. It was a watershed moment in Afghanistan cricket.

 

The APL 2021 is made up of five teams, each named after a different area of Afghanistan. The Afghanistan Cricket League piqued the interest of local fans. They were glued to numerous stations to watch the Afghanistan Premier League live score. To far, there has only been one edition of APL, which took place in 2018. Following that, ACB was unable to organise the event due to financial concerns and management crises. They are, however, making every effort to schedule APL 2021 and continue this homegrown 20 event. Fans are anxiously anticipating the next Afghanistan Premier League T20 match.
