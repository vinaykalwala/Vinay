Cognitive psychology

Cognitive psychology is the scientific study of human cognition, which includes all of our mental capacities such as perception, learning, memory, thinking, reasoning, and comprehending. The term "cognition" comes from the Latin word cognoscere, which means "to know." Cognitive psychology is the study of how humans learn and utilize knowledge or information. It is strongly connected to cognitive science, which is very multidisciplinary, and is influenced by artificial intelligence, computer science, philosophy, anthropology, linguistics, biology, physics, and neuroscience.

Developmental psychology

Developmental psychology, commonly referred to as Human Development, is the academic study of the gradual psychological alterations that take place in growing adults. It used to focus on young children and newborns, but has now expanded to include all stages of life, including puberty and aging. This discipline studies change in a variety of areas, including as problem-solving skills, conceptual understanding, language learning, moral understanding, and identity development, in addition to motor skills and other psycho-physiological processes.

Transcendental Meditation

The practice of transcendental meditation (TM) helps people stop thinking about things that are distracting and cultivate a calm awareness. In the 1960s, late Maharishi Mahesh Yogi introduced the method to the United States. A TM practitioner meditates by sitting comfortably, closing their eyes and speaking aloud a mantra. Mantras, which are words or sounds, are used in the Vedic religion to improve concentration.

What is Spiritual meditation

Fundamentally, spiritual meditation is the conscious act of connecting with something greater, larger, and deeper than the specific self. Though it may seem counter-intuitive, the way to such a connection requires sincere introspection. Several meditation methods aim to raise spiritual consciousness, but they all need honesty and sincerity when it comes to how we perceive ourselves and the world. Meditation is practised in many ways according to various religions. Anyone can participate in a guided spiritual meditation, making mindful practice open to all faiths and religions. The benefits of spiritual meditation spread outward; as our awareness and spiritual security grow, so do our desire and capacity to help others.
