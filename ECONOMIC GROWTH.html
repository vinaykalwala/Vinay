ECONOMIC GROWTH

 

What is Economic Growth?

 

A country's value of all products and services generated during a given time compared to a previous one is referred to as its economic growth rate. It is presented as a percentage. A measure of an economy's relative health over time is its rate of economic growth. The measure is typically compiled and released twice a year, quarterly.

 

The economic growth rate, in most circumstances, provides an estimate of the variation in a nation's gross domestic product (GDP). In all those nations whose economies rely substantially on foreign profits, the gross national product (GNP) may be utilized. Net revenue from overseas investments is factored into the GNP.

The wealth or resource basis of an area is referred to as the economy. The creation and consumption of goods and services in a nation can be added to the concept of the economy. When this economy expands, there is economic growth. The participants create and consume more when the economy grows.

 

GDP is the primary indicator of economic growth. Gross domestic product (GDP) is one of the most often used metrics of economic growth. GDP measures the value that manufacturing products and services adds to an economy. Economic growth is frequently measured on the basis of GDP growth.

 

Economic growth definition

Economic growth refers to the process through which a nation's economy develops over time, increasing the value of the goods and services produced. The increase in a country's Gross Domestic Product (GDP), which reflects better income and higher living conditions for its population, is a typical way to quantify it.

 

What types of economic growth are there?

 

Economic growth may be divided into two categories: intense economic growth and extensive economic growth. Extensive growth measures economic expansion on a scale of labor and human resources available, whereas intensive growth measures it on a scale of output. Let's thoroughly comprehend both subjects.

 

1.Rapid Economic Growth

 

Intensive economic growth measures an economy's growth by examining its entire production. Intensive economic growth scholars contend that economic growth occurs when production rises during a certain time period in comparison to a prior equivalent time period. Karl Marx initially proposed the concept of intense economic development, which is strongly tied to socialism. At the period, crops were the primary form of production.

 

2.Significant Economic Growth

 

The production resources that are accessible are considered in extensive economic expansion. For instance, it examines the available technology, human resources, or even viability. In that respect, it is argued that economic growth has been attained when, compared to a comparable era, the variables that support it have grown.

 

Significant economic growth is growth resulting from an increase in resources such as labour, money, natural resources, etc. Contrarily, intense economic growth is expansion brought on by increases in labour and capital productivity, as well as the effective use of resources.

 

That is the primary difference between the two. Extensive expansion just refers to a numerical increase, whereas intense growth relates to the efficient utilisation of all resources in an economy and the benefit that is realised. Rapid economic development is possible with technical advancement.

 

Evaluation of economic growth

 

Growth in the economy helps Sub-Saharan African nations emerge from the deepest depths of poverty. A little amount of economic development can help raise living standards and lengthen life expectancy. Economic growth is less important in developed countries.

Depending on the type of economic expansion. For instance, if economic expansion causes an increase in traffic and pollution, living standards may decline.

 

Who gains from economic growth? This also relies on how it is distributed. Growth might not significantly reduce poverty if it just helps the wealthiest members of society.

Environmental damage brought on by economic expansion can be severe. If it results in increased carbon emissions and the usage of non-renewable resources, it will contribute to environmental issues that might be quite serious and that will progressively affect future generations.

 

Economic growth has a large marginal benefit in nations with low GDP. Nevertheless, the marginal advantage of economic expansion is less for industrialised nations with large GDPs. The marginal value of additional money is decreasing, and at greater levels, the drawbacks of expansion may exceed the advantages.

 

The advantages of economic expansion include

 

Increased average salaries. Increased consumption of products and services and higher living standards are made possible by economic expansion. During the 20th century, economic expansion had a significant role in lowering absolute poverty levels and permitting an increase in life expectancy.

 

Fewer jobless people. Positive economic growth and increased output tend to lead to businesses hiring more people, which increases employment.

 

Reduced public debt. Increased tax revenues result in less of a requirement for spending on benefits like jobless compensation. As a result, economic development contributes to lower government borrowing. Increasing the economy also helps to lower debt-to-GDP ratios.

Higher living standards may result from this, including longer life expectancies, higher literacy rates, and a better grasp of social and political concerns.

 

The environment may be protected through financial resources. A society may allocate more resources to encouraging recycling and the use of renewable resources with stronger economic growth. However, greater expansion may be compatible with better environmental consequences.

 

Investment. Investment by businesses is encouraged by economic expansion in order to fulfil future demand. A virtuous cycle of economic development and investment is created when investment levels rise, increasing the potential for future economic growth.

 

Increased development and research. High economic growth increases business profitability, allowing for greater investment in R&D. Technological innovations such as better healthcare and environmentally friendly technologies may result from this. Additionally, continued economic expansion boosts confidence and motivates businesses to innovate and take risks.

 

Economic expansion. Sustained economic growth is the main element supporting economic development. Over the past few decades, south-east Asia's economic progress has significantly contributed to the region's decline in poverty, rise in life expectancy, and expansion of its economic opportunities.

 

More options. Economic expansion makes it possible for individuals to work in the service sector, industry, and a wider variety of lifestyles in less developed nations where a major section of the population is engaged in agriculture and subsistence farming.

 

Absolute poverty declines. Absolute povertydefined as having inadequate money to cover basic needshas significantly decreased as a result of economic progress.

Reasons for economic expansion

 

The improvement in productivity, which is the effectiveness with which resources (such as labour and capital) are utilised to generate products and services, is at the heart of economic growth. This productivity development is often fueled by a number of interrelated variables, each of which helps an economy create more output from the same amount of input. Here, we examine a few of these fundamental drivers of economic expansion.

 

Investment in tangible assets

 

The development of physical capital, such as equipment, structures, and infrastructure, is essential. By making additional capital expenditures in factories, machinery, or technology, businesses may boost their output and deliver more products and services. For instance, building new roads and railroads in India has accelerated that nation's economic development.

 

Development of Human Capital

 

The skills, knowledge, and experience that a person or population has are referred to as human capital. Investments in healthcare, education, and training may increase labour productivity and stimulate the economy. For instance, South Korea's concentration on education and skill development has played a crucial role in its transformation within a few decades from a developing to a developed economy.

 

Technological Advancement

 

Technological advancement may significantly boost economic growth by increasing productivity and opening up new business options. For instance, the introduction of the internet has revolutionised industries everywhere and made a huge contribution to the expansion of the international economy.
