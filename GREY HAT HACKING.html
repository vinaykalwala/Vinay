GREY HAT HACKING AND IT’S MOTIVE

What is a Grey hat hacker?

A hacker who falls in between the white hat and black hat classifications is known as a grey hat hacker. While Grey hat hackers occasionally reveal vulnerabilities to the affected parties or the entire public, they occasionally use vulnerabilities for their own personal advantage.

There are exceptions to any generalization, which is why the idea of "Grey hat" hackers also exists. Hackers that use Grey hats fall in between "good" and "bad." They are the ones that examine a system for flaws without authorization but maybe with good intentions. They could let a company know that they have found a way to abuse their system and then demand payment to have it fixed.

The Grey Hat hacker may, however, turn into a Black Hat hacker by publishing the point of exploitation online for everyone to see or even by exploiting the vulnerability themselves if the organisation does not reply promptly or if they do not comply at all. Because a Grey Hat hacker does not have authorization to attempt to break into a system, their work is technically illegal regardless of their motivations.

Various kinds of grey hat hackers

Grey hat hackers may be divided into two categories: those who hack for personal gain and those who hack both for personal gain and to strengthen security. The latter is occasionally referred to as a "white hat with a black heart." A contentious behavior that blurs the lines between lawful and illicit conduct is 'grey hat hacking'. Grey hat hackers' motives

Depending on the category, grey hat hackers have different goals. People that hack for their own benefit could be driven by money or just enjoy the challenge. There are a variety of reasons why people hack for both their own benefit and to increase security, including the desire to make the world a better place or to stop future assaults. Those that hack for political purposes also exist. They can aim to overthrow a corrupt organisation or government. Additionally, they can just do it for the excitement of it.

Grey Hat Hacker Examples

A security researcher by the name of Justin Shafer identified a weakness in the Electronic Health Records (EHR) system used by a sizable healthcare organization in 2016. This incident is one real-world instance of a grey hat hacker in the healthcare sector. Without the required authority, Shafer was able to access the system and discovered that it held private patient data, including medical records and social security numbers.

Shafer informed the healthcare organization about the problem and volunteered to help solve it rather than trying to exploit the vulnerability or harm the system.

Shafer notified the company, but when they didn't reply, he ultimately posted about the vulnerability on his own blog.

Despite Shafer's good intentions, several others found his access to the EHR system without authority to be unethical. Additionally, the healthcare facility could have chosen to conceal the vulnerability to prevent bad press or legal ramifications.

Shafer's revelation finally prompted the healthcare organisation to take measures to enhance the security of its EHR system, notwithstanding the criticism surrounding his conduct. In this way, the security of the system and the safeguarding of patient data were eventually favourably influenced by Shafer's efforts as a grey hat hacker.
