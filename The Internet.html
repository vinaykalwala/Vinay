DIGITAL ECONOMY

Back in the 1990s, when the internet was still a supplement to analog goods and services, the phrase "digital economy" first appeared. The main concern at that time was how developing digital channels may affect consumers and companies in the larger economy. The term "digital economy" today simply refers to any economic activity that takes place online. The capacity to gather, utilize, and analyze enormous volumes of machine-readable data in order to provide more individualized and meaningful experiences has actually led to the emergence of a data-driven economy.
