Cloud computing :

Cloud computing is a new technology that distributes a wide variety of services through the Internet. The Internet is a network of networks that provides the software and hardware infrastructure to build and maintain connectivity of computers across the world. So, a system that leverages the Internet as a communication channel to offer its services may be referred to as cloud computing. Although cloud services may be made available within businesses through LANs, cloud computing actually cannot function internationally without the Internet. The processing and distribution of various online services over the internet is known as cloud computing. Cloud computing makes it possible to access computer system resources, such as processing power and data storage, without requiring the user to actively manage them directly. In plainer terms, it is the pay-as-you- go internet delivery of on-demand computing services, such as apps, data storage, etc.

A company requires a method of information exchange if it has more than a few employees. Think of a flower shop with twenty staff members. The person who receives phone orders, the delivery guy, and the bookkeeper all require access to the client database of the business. Now, everyone could share the one computer the business had. However, it is more likely that there are numerous computers (one for delivery, one for bookkeeping, and others for salesmen). Everyone in this situation must ensure that each time a modification is necessary, customer records have been updated on all systems. In the past, the company would set up a network (LAN) so that the various computers could communicate with one another and share data.

In the present situation on the internet cloud computing is one of the demand access that many services it may be any service virtual services physical services, data storage, development tools, and all network capabilities you can access data remotely that is managed by a cloud services provider.

The technology that makes clouds function is often referred to as "cloud computing." This comprises some type of virtualized IT infrastructure, such as servers, operating systems, networking, and other infrastructure that has been abstracted using specialized software to allow pooling and dividing without regard to actual hardware boundaries.
