E-MAIL MARKETING

What is email marketing?

Email marketing is a direct marketing method via which firms may share new goods, sales, and updates with their contact list. Its strong return on investment (ROI) makes it critical to the entire inbound strategy of most firms.

Modern email marketing has shifted away from mass mailings and towards permission, segmentation, and customization. This may appear to be time-consuming, but marketing automation does the majority of the heavy lifting for you. In the long term, a well-designed email marketing campaign not only increases sales, but also contributes to the development of a community around your business.

Why Is Email Marketing Important?

Email is an old technology. It was, in fact, one of the very first forms of digital communication to appear in 1971. However, even after 50 years, email marketing is more popular than ever.

You may be wondering, "Do people still use email?" Isn't social media the place to go for marketing these days?" While social media is a critical component of any digital marketing plan, email has significant advantages.

For starters, email marketing initiatives may be more personalised than social media advertising. Following that, prices are significantly cheaper than for other channels, particularly when considering the reach and conversion rate connected with email marketing. This is one of the reasons why email marketing is so effective.

Finally, what makes email marketing so effective and profitable is that it provides you with direct, unique access to your target audience's inboxes.

The Basics of Email Marketing

Let's start with the basics before digging into the techniques you'll use to construct and use email marketing. These pointers will help you make the most of your email marketing campaign, which we'll discuss next.

Maintain Humanity:

Email marketing is popular, which means it is also competitive. Use email to communicate directly with your users, using their names and allowing them to see the human side of your company.

Use engaging Subject headings, But Don't Bait And Switch: Using engaging subject headings is critical to increase email open rates, but keep them on topic and non-spammy. Users that feel misled are more likely to unsubscribe or designate your communication as spam, reducing deliverability.

Make Messages Brief:

Because most email is viewed on a mobile device, make your text brief and to the point. If you need to convey a lot of information, direct them to a blog post or landing page.

Include CTAs at the top and bottom of the page: CTAs are always featured above the fold on landing sites; employ the same method with email by placing strong CTAs after the first paragraph and again at the conclusion.

Request Permission and Keep Your Promises:

Never buy email lists—it's unlawful in most circumstances and won't provide results. Make sure to present high-quality material or deals if you offer a discount or fantastic content.

What is the meaning of email list segmentation?

Email list segmentation is the act of categorizing your subscribers into smaller groups based on specified criteria in order to send them more personalized and relevant emails.

Rather than sending every email to your whole email list, segmentation allows you to send specific emails solely to subscribers you believe would be most interested in that material, resulting in increased conversions.
