What is Global warming?

Introduction

Global Warming is the rapid rise in the average temperature of the Earth’s surface. Global warming has some adverse effects on the climate, as well as natural resources like depletion of glaciers, causing ocean water levels to increase, changing the weather cycle, and affecting biodiversity. Simply put, none of the effects that global warming has on the earth is good and hence, measures must be taken to understand and reduce Global warming to reduce its effects. In the past few decades, many factors have contributed to Global Warming and this has caused the average temperature to rise by several degrees. While it may seem perfectly normal, a rise in average temperature may cause many low-temperature favored species to die, affect the coastal areas due to water rise by melting glaciers, and many health issues for us humans as well.

This article is a complete guide that features the causes, impacts, and ways to tackle the issue and reverse its effects.

Causes of Global Warming

There is not a single but multiple factors contributing to Global warming that need to be identified to reduce its effects on the environment.

1.    Greenhouse Effect and the Role of greenhouse gasses

The Greenhouse effect and the emission of greenhouse gases play a major role in Global Warming. The greenhouse effect is when the heat emitted by the Sun gets trapped in the Earth’s atmosphere by a layer of gasses known as greenhouse gasses. This is called so because it is similar to greenhouses made to store plants but here, the glass structure is replaced by gasses like Carbon Dioxide, Nitrous Oxide, Methane, and water vapor contaminations. Carbon Dioxide, which is a naturally occurring, non-polluting gas has a warming effect that helps stabilize the earth’s atmosphere, and removing it may lead to a fall in the greenhouse effect and reduce the global temperature by up to 33°C.

2.    Gaseous emissions from combustion of fossil fuels

Fossil fuels are the most widely used natural resource over the decades. With the increasing demand for electricity, private vehicles, and consumption of fossil fuels have increased as well. The combustion of fossil fuels like coal, petroleum, and natural gas emits a huge amount of carbon dioxide and other gasses like Methane and other Oxides that contribute to the Greenhouse effect and hence, global warming.

3.    Deforestation and loss of carbon sinks

Plants, while in photosynthesis, emit Oxygen and consume Carbon Dioxide which helps in the reduction of Carbon Dioxide levels in the atmosphere, Cutting these trees down, or causing deforestation may result in reduced photosynthesis, and hence, increased Global Warming. A carbon sink refers to a group of trees that absorb carbon from its surroundings. Deforestation by humans leads to the loss of these carbon sinks as well, large carbon skinks or forests have been reduced, and the natural balance is distorted, resulting in increased carbon dioxide levels in the atmosphere.

4.    Methane emissions from livestock and agriculture

Living entities like humans, animals, and even plants emit a gas called Methane which is a major constituent of Greenhouse gasses and reduces the quality of the air we breathe. It is released into the atmosphere from agricultural activities such as ruminant digestion, rice production, and manure management, and is many times more effective in trapping heat than carbon dioxide. Therefore, through the extensive rearing of livestock, increases in demand for food production, and poor waste management practices, the quantity of methane released into the atmosphere continues to steadily rise.

Impacts of Global Warming

Global warming is a serious natural issue that many countries are now worried about. This is because of the deadly effects it has on the environment such as-

1.    Rising temperatures and heatwaves

Global Warming is reasonable for an increase in the Global temperature of the Earth. This increased temperature results in frequent heatwaves all around, which can cause fatal heat strokes, and forest fires, impact natural resources and cause damage to infrastructures as well. Thus, the effects of Global Warming can be fatal to living as well as non-living forms.

2.    Melting ice caps and rising sea levels

The Northern and Southern poles of the Earth have millions of Tons of water frozen in the form of ice. The increase in global temperature has been causing these blocks of ice, called glaciers to melt into water. The melting of these glaciers has increased sea levels and put the coastal areas at risk of submerging underwater, a decrease in livable land area, and a risk of overpopulation as well.

3.    Changes in rain cycles and climatic imbalance

The natural precipitation depends on the natural climate of any area and the disruption of the natural ways and temperature can cause changes in these precipitation patterns. Sometimes it may mean lower precipitation and hence droughts that can lead to famine or floods that can leave many unsheltered and homeless.

4.    Disturbed ecosystems and poor biodiversity

Not all living species are built in a way that they can adjust to all living conditions and temperatures. Some were made for the lower temperatures while some for the higher ones. The changes in their living temperatures may cause them to be endangered or even extinct. And the loss of any organism from the food chain may disrupt biodiversity and harm the ecosystem in many ways.

5.    Health Effects on Humans and Wildlife

The main cause of Global Warming is mostly the actions of humans and they are affected by this as well. Global Warming has been found to affect humans and animals equally as the environment. It may cause health effects like allergies, respiratory diseases, and water-related diseases in humans which can be quite difficult to heal from. On the other hand, animals may suffer from the risk of extinction, reduced food availability, and loss of habitat.

Global Warming affects every aspect of the environment, be it living, or non-living. The impacts of Global warming are becoming more serious by the day and this needs to be controlled before it is too late for the environment.

Scientific Consensus on Global Warming

Global warming, also known as climate change, is the result of the steadily increasing temperatures of the Earth's atmosphere due to the greenhouse effect. Global Warming is a widely debated topic, but the scientific community has a clear consensus on its existence and human influence. The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) has stated in their reports that it is "extremely likely" that human activities have been the dominant cause of Global Warming observed since the mid-20th century. This claim is supported by extensive research and scientific evidence compiled over many decades. While there are still some who deny or doubt the reality of Global Warming, the overwhelming majority of scientists agree that it is a serious issue that requires immediate attention and action.

Individual Actions to Mitigate Global Warming

While many actions are being taken on a Global scale by National and International agencies, the deep-rooted problem of Global Warming needs individual actions as well. With dedicated and strict individual actions to tackle the problem, Global Warming can be reduced by many times and it can save the ecosystem.

1.    Energy consumption and conservation at home

Reducing the consumption of fossil fuels at the domestic level can help to decrease the emission of Greenhouse gases. When this decreases, it will also lead to a decrease in the Greenhouse effect and hence, overall Global Warming.

2.    Transitioning to renewable energy sources

Coal, Petroleum, LPG, and CNG are some commonly used household energy sources that we have been using for decades. However, these energy sources are non-renewable and emit harmful gases that contribute to Global Warming. Thus, it is suggested that there be a shift to renewable sources of energy such as solar energy, tidal energy, or Wind energy sources that work on renewable resources like Sunlight, Tides, or Wind and pose no threat to the environment with their byproducts.

3.    Sustainable transportation choices

Automobiles usually run on non-renewable resources like Petrol or CNG, which contribute to Global Warming and are expensive too. To reduce this, the best ways are to lower the use of personal vehicles and rather go for public transportation or carpooling. This may not completely wipe out the effects of Petrol and CNG but will surely reduce them. Also, many electrical vehicles are making their way into the market that can be electrically charged, require no petrol or diesel, emit no harmful byproducts, and are safe for the environment as well. Opting for these sustainable vehicles will reduce Global Warming to a great extent.

4.    Reducing, reusing, and recycling practices

All living animals on the planet produce certain waste, be it biological, lifestyle waste, or anything else. The best way to save the environment is to manage this waste. There are 3 types of Waste Management, Reducing the production of wastes that may harm the environment, reusing certain things that can be reused before throwing them into waste and recycling the waste generated. With these 3 waste management techniques, a significant change in Global Warming can be noticed by everyone.

5.    Raising awareness and advocating for change

Even when Global Warming has been wreaking havoc on biodiversity, not many people are aware of these issues. Every human, because they directly or indirectly contribute to this phenomenon must be made aware of what Global Warming is, and how to decrease its effect to save the planet and create a sustainable lifestyle.

Challenges and Barriers to Addressing Global Warming

While the methods to combat Global Warming are not so difficult, there still lay some barriers to reaching the cause. These barriers have the potential to stop individuals from addressing Global Warming and need to be handled carefully to reach the goal.

1.    Political and economic interests

With various political and economic groups in power, they all have different motives and interests. This difference in motives may cause individuals or groups to feel conflicted and hinder their way to doing the right. Such political and economic groups must ensure that even though they have a difference of opinion, they must still agree to the universally correct thing, and do what is right for the population.

2.    Technological and infrastructural limitations

The steps that can lower Global Warming are often thought to be technologically and infrastructurally demanding which makes people feel limited and demotivated to help decrease the greenhouse effect. In such cases, it is best that they know the alternate solutions to the issue that do not need any high-end technologies like simply limiting their energy consumption or switching to less polluting alternatives.

3.    International cooperation and conflicting priorities

Sometimes the objectives to tackle Global Warming issues are not internationally cooperated and this leads to internal as well as external conflicts. Not every government or political organization agrees to cooperate with certain causes and this may lead to delay or deception from the cause to save the environment from Global Warming.

4.    Public Perception and Denialism

While many individuals or sections of the public know and understand the effects Global Warming has on the Earth, some do not. They perceive it as a temporary issue that would subside on its own and does not matter. They are in constant denial of the pressing matter and refuse to see anything but their benefits. This attitude is one of the main barriers to solving the issue and needs to be changed to fix this problem, permanently.

5.    Overcoming the inertia of established systems

Many established systems such as textile industries, automobile industries, and incinerators have set foot in the system a bit too well. These industries themselves know the hazards they are putting the environment into but would not change their ways or close up their business. Such systems are a much-fixed barrier to overcoming Global Warming.

Future Outlook and Solutions

Global warming is a complex issue that requires expert solutions. One promising solution is the use of renewable energy sources such as wind, solar, and hydroelectric power. Governments and businesses need to invest in these technologies and transition away from fossil fuels. Additionally, individuals can do their part by reducing their consumption and investing in energy-efficient appliances. Also, it is crucial to acknowledge that industries play a significant role in exacerbating the problem. Encouraging these industries to transition to cleaner energy sources and implementing regulations to limit their emissions is a necessary step in addressing the issue. Furthermore, investing in research and development towards breakthrough technologies and innovations can help us find more efficient and sustainable ways to power our world. It is important to remember that while there is no one solution to global warming, collaborative efforts between governments, businesses, and individuals can create a more sustainable future for all

Conclusion

In conclusion, global warming is one of the greatest challenges facing humanity today. The effects of rising temperatures and environmental degradation are already being felt around the world, from more frequent natural disasters to the extinction of various species. However, by working collaboratively and implementing changes on both the individual and industrial levels, we can mitigate these impacts and create a more sustainable future. It is up to all of us to take action and make a difference, as the future of our planet depends on our collective efforts.
