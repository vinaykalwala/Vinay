HOW THE INTERNET AFFECT THE YOUNG PEOPLE

What is the Internet and how does it affect young people?

Today, a large number of young people utilize the Internet to create and share materials intended to inform, uplift, and inspire their peers on a variety of subjects, including but not limited to social justice, economics, politics, spirituality, the environment, and technology. Now, a variety of young people may learn about how to approach significant issues from many angles and minimize the impact of the numerous challenges they encounter on a daily basis as a consequence. Consider the blogs of Francis Wachira, Morning Awaits, which focuses on spiritual development, and Cynthia Nyongesa's Cynthia Untamed, which focuses on youth empowerment.

Adolescent talents and interests are profoundly impacted by immersion in a media-rich world. According to a study, people now process information and think differently than they did in the past. For research, education, and communication, they rely on media technologies. As a result, media affects their propensities for learning and social interaction. Additionally, they seek to develop the abilities needed to make creative multimedia presentations. Media multitasking has become commonplace. Teenagers are continuously checking their email, browsing the web, updating their status on Facebook, and sending texts.

Without a doubt, the Internet is today's most significant mass medium. Over a billion people use the Internet worldwide, with young people making up the majority, according to studies conducted by the Central Intelligence Agency in 2006. More than 90% of young people use computers frequently (100%) regularly use the internet.

Most young people use the Internet to interact with their friends after school, but they also use it to some extent to complete their homework and conduct research. They converse via chat rooms, emails, and wall posts on their pals. Young people use the internet so often that many experts, academics, and psychologists have started looking at how it affects young people's life.

Internet's Impact on Young People

The Internet and other new communication technologies have been determined to have a greater influence on young people's lives than any previous technological advancements. Parents and educators are worried about children lose social skills as a result of so much social interaction occurring online.

Obesity can result from a lack of exercise caused by spending hours in front of a computer, especially when using the internet. Long periods of sitting can also lead to poor posture and bodily discomfort, which reduces the effectiveness of blood flow to the legs and other body components. Occasionally, after spending a lot of time in front of the computer, Eyesight loss is also possible. Many young individuals who spend a lot of time in front of computers start to exhibit indications of illnesses that are often not noticeable until later in life.

Time spent in front of a computer may have a variety of effects. Today's youth can connect online and communicate with peers growing up in other areas of the world. Their brains are actually opened up to the possibilities of global culture by the internet, which helps them recognize and value their variety.

A very timid person who finds it difficult to engage in face-to-face communication might spend hours online conversing with someone who may be experiencing a similar issue at home. Young people can thus use the internet to discover solutions to problems they might not feel comfortable asking their parents, friends, or even professors.

Negative effects on Young People

The internet is a two-edged sword with both positive and terrible sides. To teach their children about the ambiguity in life, parents should utilize the internet as a teaching tool.

Some studies have even shown that youngsters who use computers often might enhance their visual, spatial, and reading abilities.

Computers now only have one drawback: young people can use them alone, away from their parents' attentive eyes, and some material accessible away from parents' watchful eyes could not be beneficial for young people. The only way to prevent this is for parents to keep an eye on their children without stifling their increasing freedom. The younger mind may not be able to digest the information in the right way.

Taking Their Confidence Away

Young people are so hooked on the internet that they have lost the ability to think independently. Young people in today's society sometimes use dishonest techniques like plagiarism when completing assignments in order to avoid having to rely on themselves. As a result, manual labor declines, and laziness rises.

Causes of Abuse of the Rights of Humans

People of the opposite gender who have been subjected to online harassment have reported experiencing despair as a result of the derogatory language used by young people to disseminate information via social media.

Participates in Online Crime

The Internet has developed into a haven for young individuals who want to conduct cybercrime. Young people utilizing the Internet to perpetuate crimes like stealing money from other people's accounts or getting access to private information for financial gain or public humiliation, there has been an increase in cybercrime. This has led to many young people getting into legal difficulties, and they frequently run the possibility of going to jail if they are detected.

Establishes Jabbering

Online gambling and other types of gambling are popular among young people nowadays. Teenagers have lost a lot of money and even killed themselves.

Positive Effects on Young People

Promoting Innovation and Creativity Today

The increased Internet penetration among today's youth has allowed innovations like smartphone applications (like Branch and Tala) that provide quick credit access viable. Additionally, through spending time online and consuming material, young people are creating fresh answers to long-standing social issues. Young people have recently pushed their government to preserve their rights and create social justice for all through the use of the Internet.

The importance of the Internet has increased recently, especially in academic contexts, as more and more young people are able to read and study resources that are available online, like books, from the comfort of their own homes. As a result, pupils have easier access to more resources and are therefore more likely to succeed in school.

Encourage the exploitation of morals and character

Many young individuals squander their time online watching pornographic video content and other films and videos that are bad for their heads. Particularly when it comes to early sex, which has negative consequences for their entire lives, this has taken advantage of their morality and character. When young viewers want to mimic, this practice has the greatest detrimental impact.

Social media experiences of teenagers

Teenagers may now instantaneously interact with others and share their life through images, videos, and status updates thanks to social media. These platforms are cited by teens as essential for building and sustaining connections, being creative, and expanding their worldviews. However, they also have to deal with some of the more unfavorable effects of social media use, such as drama, bullying, and the need to project a specific image.

HOW TEENS ARE REALLY AFFECTED BY THE INTERNET

It has grown in popularity and become a mainstay of human activity ever since the first computer building blocks of the internet were created in the middle of the 1960s. The teenage population of today, or Generation Z, best exemplifies this. Particularly considering that teenagers may easily access these networks from their smartphones everywhere they go. The average age of someone purchasing a smartphone is just 10 years old, and it does 't take long for these young children to learn how to connect with others using these phones, too, according to a 2017 Nielsen survey. A 2018 survey by the group Common Sense Media found that 33%–39% of US teens say texting is their preferred smartphone use.

Teenagers appear to use YouTube, Instagram, and Snapchat the most often among social media platforms. In a 2018 Pew Research Centre poll, youths in the US reported using YouTube 85% of the time, Instagram 72% of the time, and Snapchat 69% of the time. The majority of US teenagers use social media, and according to the Pew Research Centre, they have differing opinions on how it has impacted their lives. Social media, according to 31% of US teenagers, has a primarily beneficial influence on individuals their age, while 24% of teenagers feel it has a mostly negative impact. The primary justifications for a positive influence were forming connections with family and friends. whereas bullying and relationship damage brought on by a lack of in- person interaction was cited as the primary factor for a negative influence. It's interesting to note that, according to 45% of kids surveyed by Pew Research, social media has neither a positive nor negative impact on individuals their age. So it appears that Gen Z has a mixed opinion of social media after all.
