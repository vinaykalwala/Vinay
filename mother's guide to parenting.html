Navigating the Teenage Years: A Mother's Guide to Parenting Adolescents

 

     I.           Introduction

Raising adolescents might sound relaxing to some but in reality, can be quite intimidating as they go through physical, mental, emotional roller coasters during the teen years. Some struggle due to their appearance while others might be growing in unhealthy environments both of which tragically damage their mental health. This can also happen if they feel their independence or privacy is being threatened. What teen boys and girls need in this period of their lives is a supportive family and to be able to communicate with their loved ones without hesitation.

Adolescence is a challenging period for the child’s as well as the parents as they have to deal efficiently with the child's tantrums without making them feel like they’reunlovedd or misunderstood. This also creates a good relationship between the teen and the parents.

Understanding and keeping up with their needs is by far one of the most important things that will help your child feel emotionally supported by your actions.

1.     Communication

Being verbal with your adolescent children about their likes, dislikes, feelings, and generally about their life and making them feel like home and avoiding criticism is key to having a nice experience parenting them.

2.     Free will

Giving them the power of making their own decisions but not letting them out of grasp will not only make your kid feel responsible but will also develop a sense of trust and gratitude towards you.

3.     Emotional support

In this stage of their lives, children have to endure a lot of criticism and hate from society, you being their safe space in which they can be vulnerable about their feelings will help them mentally, emotionally and will make them feel grateful for being understood.

4.     Digital World

Sitting down with them once in a while and educating them about the pros and cons of the digital world will also provide you a medium through which you can be involved in their lives.

   II.           Setting Boundaries and Rules

Having a clear conversation about the importance of setting their own boundaries will help them in many ways in their day to day lives. It will not only save them from bad company but will also get them to invest their time in something valuable leading to a healthy and happy life. Teens in this phase are still developing their views of the outer world and exploring it. It's normal for them to cross paths with some sketchy people who may lead them to practise unhealthy activity or just plainly run their precious time by showing them false hopes or faking their feelings for your child. In order to maintain their emotional stability, parents need to teach them how to set healthy boundaries and not keep high expectations from anyone but always give their best in any activity they do.

III.           Building Trust and Respect

Your adolescents need to feel safe to be vulnerable around you, and if they’re choosing not to, then one will need to carry out activities in order to develop trust in their minds.Building and Maintaining trust and respect can be a great aspect in fostering teens with healthy minds. Here are some ways to do the same:

1.     Active Listening

Devote your full attention towards your teenager when they speak to you. Have opinions about the information they share but don't be too fixated in proving your point and asserting dominance over their opinions. Show genuine interest in what they have to say even if it's silly, and have a good laugh with them about it in the future years.

2.     Be supportive

Show your teenager that you will be by their side no matter what the scenario is. Show them support in their career, goals, dreams, and activity choices. Be there for them when they need guidance without having rigid opinions and being fixated on your ideology of life.

3.     Physical Changes

Educating your teens on their drastically changing bodies and minds will be quite helpful to make them feel comfortable about the changes they’re undergoing. Make sure to not be judgemental or ashamed about this topic since it’s really sensitive.

4.     Peer pressure from society

Having them know that what people say about them is just a reflection of what those people feel about themselves will make your child see the world from a different perspective and will save them a lot of time trying to figure out what’s wrong.

IV.           Supporting Emotional Well-being

 

Address common emotional challenges teenagers face daily. The emotional gland is at it’s peak during teenage years. This can make understanding what they feel somewhat difficult as adults might not exactly feel what the teenagers do. But still, you need to show them love, care, and support. Some common areas where emotions are stored in the body include the neck, shoulders, back, hips, and stomach. Discuss ways to support their emotional well-being, including coping with stress and emotions. Encourage open discussions about mental health and seeking professional help when needed because teenagers might not feel comfortable talking about their personal experiences, thoughts, struggles and mistakes with their parents, so it’s advised to have them seek for a professional that they feel comfortable talking to and who can help them process their emotions and guilts.

  V.           Navigating Technology and Social Media

Discussing the impact of social media on your child can be a really important aspect to get to know the mental condition of your child. Social media can be really fun as well as damaging to young folks as they believe what they see at this age and since not everything on the internet is how it looks through the screens, adolescents can develop insecurities leading to severe health conditions. Setting healthy screen-time limits and helping them build a safe and healthy social lifestyle will help you raise your teenagers.

VI.           Encouraging Independence and Responsibility

Fostering independence in adolescents is a big aspect of their mental as well as emotional development and their overall well–being.Encouraging your teenagers to be independent and make their own decisions but also navigating them will encourage them to become more responsible, confident and capable individuals.

Here are some tips for teaching your children responsibility and life skills :

 

  1. Assigning age appropriate chores

Give your children reasonable tasks around the house according to their age but remember to not be harsh on them or else they might end up feeling like housekeepers.

 

  1. Cooking skills

Involve your kids while preparing a meal to teach them basic things in cooking that may help them in times of need.

 

  1. Safety rules

Teach them the importance of keeping an eye on everything at any stance of time, this way they will learn to be more attentive about their surroundings.

 

  1. Respect and care for belongings

Fostering a sense of motherhood in your teens will help them as well as you in a lot of ways. This way they will learn to take care of not only themselves but also others around them and their belongings. But make sure to tell them not to overdo it since that can lead to misconceptions about one's intentions.

 

  1. Empathy and kindness

Showing empathy to others is going to make them come off as a reliable and good human being which will immediately boost their social image.

VII.           Handling Conflict and Discipline

 

Resolving any conflict with calmness and respect is essential in keeping relationships healthy. Here are some tips to teach your teens in order to avoid a heated conflict or argument:

 

  1. Stay calm

Studies have found that staying grounded while a disagreement is a major breakthrough to tackle messy fights. This not only eventually cools down the other person but also gives you time to think and respond.

 

  1. Think before responding

Don’t respond at the very moment as the brain stimulates the surrounding and generates a quick response to that stimulation which isn’t always the best thing to say. So keep calm and think before responding.

 

  1. Avoiding judgement

Judging someone in a heated situation is only going to make the situation worse and it’s not a good thing to judge people for something they’ve done or went through.

 

  1. Taking responsibility

 Holding yourself accountable for your actions and their consequences is a good thing to teach your children in their developing ages. Taking responsibility can sometimes be shameful as teens tend to do silly things here and there but doing so puts you out to be a secure person who's not ashamed of standing up for justice.

VIII.           Celebrating Milestones and Achievements

Celebrating tiny things that a teenager accomplishes can mean a lot to them, they feel like they are seen, appreciated and loved for who they are.This will also motivate them to keep doing activities that catch their interest and eventually help them build a secure life for themselves. Here are some ways to show kindness and empathy to your teenager:

  1. Words of encouragement

Guiding them through hard times by speaking positively about life and the situations created gives motivation to young minds encouraging them to not give up on their dreams.

 

  1. Remembering date

Remembering dates associated with important events such as graduation, their first paycheck, birthdays, and big accomplishments can make people feel loved and acknowledged and loved.

 

  1. Surprise treats and gifts

Treating and spoiling your adolescents once in a while will make them feel included in your life. It can be their favourite food from a particular restaurant, a game that they’ve been eyeing for a long time, their favourite clothing brands can make a huge impact on their minds.

IX.           Conclusion

Concluding this blog, I would like to convey a direct message to all the parents out there, not just the mom’s, not just the dad’s. Your kid is growing up to become an adult at this period of their life, meaning that their emotions resemble a hurricane, you can think it’s calming down but it’s actually on the verge of blowing up. Taking precautionary measures is advised during such times. But most importantly, acknowledge your child, love them unconditionally for who they are, accept them for their reality and their life choices, support them in their life battles and even in their joyful victories,don’t force them to believe what you believe, after all, they’re allowed to have their own perspective and opinions about things.

Dear Parents,

As you indulge in the beauty of parenthood,  encourage you to share your experiences with other parents to gain a different perspective for your situation, you can also consult with younger folks who know your child to get to their side of the story and to analyse what they’re going through that they can’t be open to you about.

The most important aspect about this journey is to never forget that you’re not alone in this journey,and connecting with others on this topic will give you joy, strength and valuable companionship along the way.
