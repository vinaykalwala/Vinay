Progressive muscular relaxation

Introduction: 

During progressive muscular relaxation it's common to have anxiety from time to time. However, if your stress builds up or persists for an extended length of time, you may carry the tension in your muscles. You may be experiencing muscular stiffness without even recognizing it.

 

Progressive muscular relaxation, often known as Jacobson's relaxation technique, is one method for relieving muscle tension. Progressive muscle relaxation (PMR) is a method of therapy that involves precisely contracting and releasing muscle units one at a time.

 

The idea is to relax your muscles while also letting you identify how that tension feels.

Progressive muscular relaxation as part of yoga.

This strategy, when used on a regular basis, may help you manage the physical impacts of stress.

 

What is Progressive Muscle Relaxation?

 

A specialized relaxation method called progressive muscle relaxation includes tensing and releasing different muscle groups all over your body, starting from your feet and working your way up to your head (or vice versa). To release tension and trigger your body's relaxation reaction is the aim. It deliberately affects your physiology's inner workings to help your body and mind release tension, stress, and issues.

 

Dr. Edmund Jacobson created progressive muscle relaxation in the 1920s on the basis of the idea that experiences that trigger the stress response in the body (such as anxiety) and relaxation are diametrically opposed processes in the body and mind. Both of these opposing processes cannot be experienced concurrently. The first formal publication of progressive relaxation as a method of relaxation was made in 1938.

 

Your nervous system "switches gears" from the tension to the relaxation response when you gradually tighten and release muscle groups all over your body. Your breathing becomes slower, your heart rate and blood pressure drop, and the stress chemicals cortisol and adrenaline are produced less.

 

What are the benefits of PMR?

 

The advantages of PMR for health are well supported by research. Let's examine what research has shown regarding the advantages of this method in more detail.

 

Lowers stress and fear

One of the main advantages of PMR is the reduction of anxiety. This comprises anxiety brought on by a stressful circumstance or generalized anxiety disorder.

 

A 2019 research study by Trusted Source of 50 jobless people revealed that PMR reduced the signs of stress, anxiety, and sadness. It also raised one's quality of life and overall sense of well-being.

 

Another 2019 study by Trusted Source found that PMR might help dental patients feel less stressed and anxious. The researchers found that PMR also assisted these individuals in lessening depression symptoms.

 

According to bigger research conducted in 2016 by Trusted Source, PMR is just as effective in reducing tension, anxiety, and rage as acupuncture.

 

Moreover, in a 2020 study according to Trusted Source, PMR may be beneficial for coping with stressful situations by lowering anxiety in COVID-19 patients.

 

Reducing neck discomfort

 

You may feel neck discomfort if you frequently hold stress in your shoulders or neck. It's a widespread illness that is frequently linked to mental and emotional strain.

 

based on a 2013 research Trusted Source: PMR may aid in easing the signs and symptoms of persistent nonspecific neck discomfort. Additionally, it could enhance bodily performance and quality of life in the process.

 

Lowers back ache

 

Low back discomfort is another typical ailment. Although there are several potential reasons, stress can exacerbate it.

 

2018 research According to a reliable source, PMR for eight weeks may lessen persistent low back pain.

 

Added research from 2014According to Trusted Source, PMR combined with music can help pregnant women with low back discomfort.

 

Improves sleep

 

Because PMR relaxes you, it could also improve your sleep.

 

In a 2020 study Trusted Source, 80 burn victims had PMR testing. Due to their psychological and physical circumstances, these patients frequently have significant levels of anxiety and poor sleep.

 

Two groups of patients were created. Three days in a row, one group engaged in PMR for 20 to 30 minutes each day. The other group only received standard medical attention.

 

The patients who had PMR demonstrated a substantial reduction in anxiety and an increase in sleep quality compared to the group who just got regular treatment after three days, the researchers found.

 

Furthermore, in a 2015 study Trusted Source, PMR assisted moms of preterm newborns in getting better postpartum sleep.

 

Systolic blood pressure is improved

 

Your risk of heart disease and stroke increases if you have hypertension, often known as high blood pressure. PMR may assist, but stress might make the issue worse.

 

A 2019 investigation According to a reliable source, music therapy and PMR helped older persons' systolic blood pressure.

 

In a 2018 research using PMR alone, it was discovered that persons with high blood pressure may noticeably lower their systolic BP.

 

However, it didn't appear to have an impact on diastolic blood pressure in either study.

 

Reduces the number of migraine episodes that occur

 

A neurological disorder called migraines makes your face and head ache terribly. Stress, especially typical everyday pressures, can cause migraine episodes.

 

A 2016 study by Trusted Source found that PMR can lessen the frequency of migraine attacks. The neurotransmitter serotonin, which is frequently low in those with migraine, is thought to aid by regulating the levels of other neurotransmitters.

 

Exercises for Progressive Muscle Relaxation in Meditation

 

Exercises for progressive muscle relaxation can be done in a variety of ways. Although this approach is one of the more popular ones, you may also experiment with other ones if you want more diversity. Other instances of practicing progressive muscle relaxation include:

 

Easing and relaxing the facial muscles

 

Concentrating on each particular body component, beginning with your feet and moving up to your head

merely letting one part of your body (such your upper or lower legs) relax at a time

Start by concentrating on the areas of your body that are the most tense, such as your shoulders or your back muscles.

No matter how stressed you are, during progressive muscular relaxation techniques can help you feel better.

They let you let go of any pent-up tension and lower stress levels. Furthermore, this form of exercise can be a simple approach to improve other aspects of one's life! Progressive muscle relaxation may appear difficult at first, but it is only a matter of effort and time to master. Try it for yourself and notice how much it improves your whole sense of well-being!
