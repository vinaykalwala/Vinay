Clinical psychology

 

Introduction:

 

Clinical psychology is a fascinating and expanding discipline. It includes both the study of psychopathology and the practice of mental health. Clinical psychologists specialize in diagnosing, treating, and preventing mental health issues. Clinical psychologists are at the forefront of the treatment of mental health issues through the use of psychological tests, interviews, observations of behaviour, and various psychological treatments (e.g., cognitive behavioral therapy, interpersonal psychotherapy, marital/family therapy).

 

The major subfield of psychology that deals with methods and techniques used outside of the lab is clinical psychology. The field of applied psychology, which encompasses organizational, educational, industrial, and a number of other subfields, is by no means all-inclusive. Based on their understanding of emotional processes and how they may be impacted by external factors including culture and environment, clinical psychologists are excellent at predicting human behaviour. They are specialists in motivating others to adopt more positive behaviours.

 

What is Clinical psychology?

 

The study of clinical psychology involves using psychological theories and methods in "clinical" situations. One straightforward definition of clinical psychology is "the study of individuals, by observation or experimentation, with the intention of promoting change." Their line of work is referred to as "clinical" since it entails interacting with and watching patients in clinics and other relevant settings. However, psychologists who practice clinical psychology may also collaborate with a group of other medical professionals or social workers. Clinicians see patients, families, and other groups at facilities including hospitals, schools, and counseling centers. They work at veteran service facilities and neighborhood health clinics.

 

The majority of consumers voluntarily seek out psychological therapy. However, clinical psychologists are occasionally chosen by courts or insurance firms to conduct psychological examinations and assessments that support legal rulings. Therefore, professional psychologists evaluate the mental health of prisoners. They must be excellent listeners, highly competent, and capable of identifying mental and psychological issues and providing treatments, regardless of the circumstance or clinical environment. Being organized is necessary for their therapeutic work because it is part of their responsibility to maintain correct records of client sessions and gather data.

 

The Fundamentals of Clinical Psychology

At its foundation, clinical psychology is a type of care that may assist people of all ages in addressing a variety of mental health issues, including learning difficulties, eating disorders, anxiety, and depression.

 

Cynthia Catchings stated that "Clinical psychology has several key aspects associated with the practical application of research methodologies and findings in the diagnosis and treatment of mental disorders." During clinical psychology therapy, the following procedures may be used:

 

Assessment and screening: According to Catchings, "This is a way of learning about the client, including diagnosing and planning treatment, that involves evaluation in order to understand what is affecting him/her/them."

 

Treatment: Individual, group, or a mix of the two therapies may be used. "The treatment modality varies from client to client, depending on their needs and personalities," Catchings further explains.

 

Discharge: Discharge is the process of removing a patient from a hospital or other institution when therapy is complete, according to Catchings, if you choose an inpatient program.

 

"A clinical psychologist can work with an individual, a family, or a group," says Catchings. However, they focus on the individual and their behaviors.

 

Catchings adds that when dealing with clients in clinical psychology, psychologists might employ a variety of therapeutic philosophies. She lists the following as the main theoretical viewpoints in clinical psychology:

 

Psychodynamic Approach: Derived from the work of Sigmund Freud, a psychodynamic approach is focused on childhood trauma and the unconscious mind.

 

Cognitive Behaviour Perspective (CBT): CBT encourages people to recognize unfavorable thought and behavior patterns while offering coping strategies and advice on how to handle challenging circumstances.

 

Perspective from a humanist: This psychological method was created in the 1960s and is still commonly employed today. It's an approach that treats each customer as a complete individual with a distinct personality that must stand out from the crowd.

 

What are the main areas of clinical psychology?

 

Let's look at some clinical psychology case studies. Individuals of all ages and from all areas of life can receive mental health treatments from clinical psychology. Techniques and methods might change from one practice to the next. Clinical psychology, however, focuses on evaluating clients' mental health through psychological testing and evaluation and offering the proper solutions. Clinical psychologists sometimes perform research and serve as consultants in addition to these main duties.  The main duties of a clinical psychologist are detailed below:

 

Assessing

 

Clinical psychologists restore mental health in the same way that physicians seek to enhance physical health. They must first identify the issue and its root cause. Therefore, the clinical psychologist evaluates the client to determine the nature of the mental health problem. There are several methods to achieve this.

 

l  During a diagnostic interview, the clinical psychologist will ask the client questions to allow them to express themselves. These inquiries delve into the client's thoughts, feelings, and behaviour as well as how the past affects the present.

l  A clinical psychologist can watch and assess a client's behaviour through a behavioral evaluation. A pattern of behaviour that suggests the existence of a mental problem or sickness may be revealed by this examination.

l  Standardized psychological tests can be used to examine a person's mental health. These are official exams that are frequently administered as checklists and questionnaires.

 

Intervening

 

The practitioner will suggest a psychological intervention, or treatment, based on what the evaluations show. There are several therapeutic philosophies. Although some clinical psychologists choose one technique over another, a client may be treated utilizing a combination of tactics. Treatments, regardless of modality, need several sessions. Following the completion of therapy, mental health maintenance programs usually involve infrequent follow-up consultations.

 

According to the cognitive behavioral approach, a person's negative beliefs and behaviors are the root cause of many mental diseases. These are frequently revealed during "talk therapy" sessions with the mental health counsellor, which entail frank discussion about possibly upsetting and unpleasant prior events. The objective is to show the client how to identify emotional triggers and teach them positive responses to them.

 

The psychodynamic technique focuses on the unconscious mind while also helping the client become aware of damaging ideas. Through psychoanalysis, the clinical psychologist assists the client in uncovering and addressing earlier troubles.

 

The humanistic method is sometimes referred to as "client-centred therapy." It encourages acceptance, sensitivity, and the notion that the client is the only one who truly understands themselves.

 

Consulting

 

In addition to treating patients, clinical psychologists routinely collaborate with other healthcare practitioners and organizations. They might be asked to assist with community health programs or to provide their expertise in other ways.

 

Researching

 

Clinical psychologists frequently consult the most recent research while spending the most of their time with clients. They could also carry out original research using the information they have gathered.

 

Conclusion:

 

In order to improve psychological and physical well-being, it uses psychological concepts to the evaluation, prevention, amelioration, and rehabilitation of psychological distress, impairment, dysfunctional behaviour, and health-risk behaviour. The area of psychology includes a wide range of clinical psychological practices and research.

 

The primary objective of clinical psychology, a discipline of psychology, is the practical application of research results and methodologies in the fields of mental and physical health.
