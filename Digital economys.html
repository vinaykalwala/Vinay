DIGITAL ECONOMY

Back in the 1990s, when the internet was still a supplement to analog goods and services, the phrase "digital economy" first appeared. The main concern at that time was how developing digital channels may affect consumers and companies in the larger economy. The term "digital economy" today simply refers to any economic activity that takes place online. The capacity to gather, utilize, and analyze enormous volumes of machine-readable data in order to provide more individualized and meaningful experiences has actually led to the emergence of a data-driven economy. Companies are now able to innovate new business models and economic value in ways that were unthinkable thirty years ago thanks to the digital economy. In fact, at least two-thirds of executives claim that digital business activities significantly aid in the accomplishment of their main corporate goals.

ECONOMIC GROWTH

A country's value of all products and services generated during a given time compared to a previous one is referred to as its economic growth rate. It is presented as a percentage. A measure of an economy's relative health over time is its rate of economic growth. The measure is typically compiled and released twice a year, quarterly. The economic growth rate, in most circumstances, provides an estimate of the variation in a nation's gross domestic product (GDP). In all those nations whose economies rely substantially on foreign profits, the gross national product (GNP) may be utilized. Net revenue from overseas investments is factored into the GNP.
