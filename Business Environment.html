Business Environment :

A business is an organization or creative entity busy with commercial, industrial, or professional activities. The environment is our surroundings or conditions where people, animals, and plants live or operate. The Business environment makes reference to the totality of external forces, factors, individuals, and institutions that are outside the control of a business enterprise but that may affect its performance. In simple words, the business environment is the surrounding in which a business exists.

The sum total of

§  All individuals, institutions, and other forces

§  That is outside the control of a business firm

§  But that may affect its performance.

For Example :

§  An increase in taxes by the government can make things costly to buy.

§  Technological improvements may render existing products new.

§  Convert to the fashions and tastes of customers may shift demand in the market from have life products to new ones.

§  Increased rivalry in the market may reduce the profit edge of hard.

“ The environment of a business be formed of all those external things to which it is exposed and by which it may be affected directly or indirectly “.

Reinecker and Schoell :

“ It surrounds the – weather or set of conditions, economic, social, political or establishment in which business working is conducted “.

Ø  It allows the firm to identify opportunities and get the first-mover advantage.

Ø  It helps the firm to identify warning and early warning signals

Ø  It helps in coping with fast changes

Ø  It helps in help in planning and policy formulation

Ø  It helps in improving performance

Ø  It provides so many various resources like machines, raw materials, capital labor, etc.

Ø  It provides a number of opportunities

Ø  It is very important to identify opportunities to improve the performance of a business.

Ø  The business environment should be monitored because it helps in coping with rapid changes.

Ø  The environment plays an important role in healthy living and the existence of life on planet Earth.

Ø  Helpful in Trapping and Assembling Resources

Ø  Importance in Performance

 

1.      Enables the firm to identify opportunities and get the first mover advantage :

§  Opportunities refer to the positive external trends or changes that will help a firm to improve its performance.

§  Environment provides a lot of opportunities for business success.

§  Early recognition of opportunities helps an enterprise to be the first to exploit them instead of losing them to competitors.

§  For example, Maruti Udyog became a leader in the small car market because it was the first to recognize the need for small cars in the population of India.

 

2.      It helps the firm to identify warning and early warning signals :

§  Warning mention to the external environment movement and changes that will hamper a hard performance.

§  Besides opportunities, the environment happens to be the source of many threats.

§  Environmental recognition can help managers to identify many warnings on time and work as an early warning signal.

§  For example, if an Indian hard finds that a foreign multinational is in the Indian market with new replacements, it should act as a warning signal.

§  On the basis of these details, Indian firms can prepare to meet the threat by taking on such measures as improving the quality of the product, reducing cost of the production, being attractive in aggressive announcements, and so on.

 

3.      It helps in manage with rapid changes :

§  Today’s business environment is getting more and more active and conversions are taking place at a fast step.

§  It is not the reality of conversation itself that is so important as the step of change.

§  Unstable market conditions, less brand fealty, divisions and subdivisions (fragmentation) of markets, more demanding consumers, fast changes in technology, and intense global competition are just a few of the images used to describe today’s business environment.

§  All sizes and all types of enterprises are facing more and more dynamic environments.

§  In order to successfully survive these important changes, managers must understand and examine the environment and develop suitable courses of action.

 

4.      It helps in assisting in planning and policy formulation :

§  Since the environment is a source of both opportunities and threats for a business enterprise, its understanding, and analysis can be the basis for deciding the future course of action (planning) or training instructions for settlement (policy).

§  For example, the entry of new players in the market, which means more rivalry may make an enterprise think afresh about how to deal with the situation.

 

5.      It helps in opening useful resources :

§  Because the business depends on the environment as an origin of inputs or resources and as a means of expression for outputs, it only makes sense that the business plan scheme allows it to get the resources that it needs so that it can convert those resources into outputs that the environment wish.

§  This can be done better by understanding what the environment had to offer.

 

Types of Business Environment :

There are 2 types of Business Environments

1. Internal Environment and 2. External Environment.

1.      Internal Environment :

§  Internal Environment is also known as controllable environment. It includes factors that are under the control of the business unit. And these factors can be changed or modified.

These factors are :

§  Organisational resources

§  Production methods

§  Marketing Capabilities

§  Managerial attitude

§  Policies of business

2.      External Environment :

The External Environment is also known as an uncontrollable environment because it includes factors that are largely beyond the control of the individual enterprise.

§  Macro Environment :

It means the environment which includes those factors with which business is closely related. These factors influence each business unit differently.

                             These factors are :

§     Customers

§     Competitors

§     Suppliers

§     Marketing intermediaries

§     Public

 

§  Macro Environment :

 

It includes factors that have distant relation with business. They influence almost all business units simultaneously. The macro forces are generally more unmanageable than the micro forces.

These factors are :

§     Political

§     Economical

§     Social

§     Technological

§     Legal

International Business Environment :

§  It is the interaction of the domestic and foreign business environment.

§  As we know that Environment keeps on changing. Thus international Business Environment is also Dynamic in nature and affected by many factors.

§  International Business Environment is composed of the economic environment, Political Environment, Legal Environment, Socio-Culture Environment, Technological Environment, and other factors which affect International Trade and Finance.

§  Economic Environment is most important for every country because it is related to Foreign Investment, International Organisations, International Trade Agreements, Economic Conditions, Economic Policies, Economic System etc.
